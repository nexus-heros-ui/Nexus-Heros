Nexus Heroes Rulebook

1. Goal of the Game

Create heroes, build teams, complete missions, fight villains, earn rewards, and grow stronger through the campaign.

2. Player and GM Roles

Players: Create heroes, make choices, fight battles, complete missions, and build teams.

GM: Controls villains, missions, rewards, story events, battles, consequences, and world rules.

3. Hero Creation

1. Choose or generate a name.
2. Spin Species, Origin, and Alignment.
3. Spin Power Chance.
4. If powers are granted, spin Power Count.
5. Spin Power Wheel once for each power slot.
6. Spin all stat wheels.
7. Spin Equipment Chance, Count, and Equipment if granted.
8. Spin Weakness Count and Weaknesses.
9. Press Generate Entire Hero.

4. Stats

Heroes have 7 stats: Strength, Speed, Durability, Intelligence, Combat, Stamina, and Power Control. Each stat ranks from 1 to 14.

5. Power Score and Rank

Power Score is all 7 stat numbers added together.

Ranks:
Street Hero → Enhanced Hero → Super Hero → Champion → Omega Hero → Cosmic Hero → Nexus Ascendant

6. How Fighting Works

Combat is turn-based. Each character gets one turn per round. On your turn, you may usually do one main action and one movement action.

7. Turn Actions

On your turn you may choose one:

Attack: Strike, shoot, blast, or use a power offensively.
Defend: Block, dodge, shield, or reduce incoming damage.
Use Power: Use a power creatively outside a normal attack.
Use Equipment: Activate a weapon, armor, relic, or artifact.
Help Ally: Give another hero an advantage.
Move: Change position, escape, chase, fly, teleport, or take cover.

8. Combat Rolls

To attack, roll or compare using the most relevant stat:

Strength for melee power.
Speed for dodging or fast attacks.
Combat for fighting skill.
Power Control for powers.
Intelligence for tactics or tech.

The GM decides the difficulty based on the enemy and situation.

9. Damage Rules

If an attack succeeds, compare the attacker’s relevant stat against the defender’s Durability, Speed, or defensive power. Stronger attacks cause heavier damage. The GM decides if damage is light, serious, critical, or battle-ending.

10. Defeat

A defeated character is not always dead. They may be knocked out, captured, forced to retreat, badly injured, or temporarily removed from battle. Death should only happen if the GM and story allow it.

11. Powers

Powers can be used in combat, missions, movement, defense, investigation, or roleplay. Strong powers still require Power Control. Overpowered powers are rare and should have GM limits.

12. Power Rarity

Common: 50%
Uncommon: 30%
Rare: 14%
Epic: 4.5%
Legendary: 1.3%
Nexus / Extreme: 0.2%

13. Equipment Rules

A hero must win the Equipment Chance Wheel before gaining equipment. If equipment is granted, spin Equipment Count, then spin Equipment once for each item. Equipment can be earned, gifted, traded item-for-item, stolen, damaged, upgraded, or destroyed if the GM allows it.

14. Weakness Rules

Weaknesses are flaws, limits, or dangers connected to the hero. The GM may use weaknesses during missions, battles, or story events. Weaknesses should make the game harder but not make the hero useless.

15. Teams

Players can form teams. Team size is normally 5 heroes max. Teams can complete harder missions, fight stronger villains, and enter PvP battles if allowed.

16. PvP Rules

PvP is allowed only if the GM allows it. Both players choose actions, compare relevant stats, and the GM decides results. PvP should be fair and not ruin the campaign.

17. Missions

The GM generates or creates missions. Missions have objectives, enemies, danger, rewards, and consequences. Winning missions can give equipment, upgrades, reputation, or story progress.

18. Villains

Villains are controlled by the GM. Villains have ranks, power scores, classes, missions, and threat levels. Stronger villains should require stronger heroes or teams.

19. Battle Simulator

The Battle Simulator compares Hero Power Score against Villain Power Score with a random battle bonus. It gives a quick result: Hero Victory, Villain Victory, or Draw.

20. Rewards

Rewards can include credits, rare equipment, epic equipment, legendary equipment, artifacts, relics, power upgrades, reputation, bonus stat points, or team resources.

21. Campaign Modes

Standard Campaign: Normal story campaign with missions and villains.

Free Campaign: Endless campaign where threats keep getting stronger.

Power Campaign: Heroes compete to become the strongest and possibly rule.

22. Trading

There is no full marketplace. Players may trade item-for-item or gift items if the GM allows it.

23. GM Final Rule

The GM has final say on unclear rules, power limits, mission results, villain actions, rewards, and campaign consequences.

Powers Reference

Enhanced Strength: Increased lifting, striking, and grappling power.
Enhanced Speed: Faster movement and reaction time.
Flight: Allows air travel and aerial combat.
Regeneration: Heals damage over time.
Invulnerability: Greatly reduces physical damage.
Telepathy: Read, sense, or send thoughts.
Telekinesis: Move objects with the mind.
Teleportation: Instantly move to another place.
Reality Warping: Change reality in GM-approved ways.
Existence Erasure: Extremely rare ability to erase a target from existence.
Nexus Authority: Highest-level Nexus energy control.

Equipment Reference

Energy Sword: Energy melee weapon.
Plasma Blade: Superheated cutting weapon.
Gravity Hammer: Creates gravity impacts.
Combat Rifle: Standard ranged weapon.
Pulse Rifle: Fires energy pulses.
Energy Shield: Creates a defensive barrier.
Power Armor: Boosts strength and defense.
Celestial Armor: Cosmic-level armor.
Reality Core: Stabilizes or alters reality effects.
World Relic: Rare artifact with GM-created world-level effects.
