NEXUS HEROES GM RULEBOOK

1. What Is Nexus Heroes?

Nexus Heroes is a build-your-own superhero tabletop game where players create heroes using wheels. The Game Master controls villains, missions, rewards, battle rules, and campaign events. The game can be played as a quick character builder, a one-shot battle game, or a long campaign.

2. Basic Game Loop

  1. Create a hero.
  2. Spin powers, stats, equipment, and weaknesses.
  3. Add heroes to a team.
  4. The GM generates a mission.
  5. The GM generates or creates a villain.
  6. Resolve the battle using quick battle, dice battle, or full campaign battle.
  7. Award rewards, upgrades, story progress, or new equipment.

3. Hero Creation

Name

Players may type their own hero name or use the Random Name button.

Species

Species determines what kind of being the character is. Examples include Human, Mutant, Alien, Synthetic, and Ascended Being. Species can be used by the GM for roleplay bonuses, story restrictions, or special background rules.

Origin

Origin explains how the hero became powerful. Examples include Scientific Accident, Magic, Technology, Cosmic Energy, and Elite Training. Origin can affect how NPCs treat the hero and what kinds of missions connect to them.

Alignment

Alignment describes morality and behavior. Heroes can be pure heroes, anti-heroes, neutral, mercenary, villain-like, or cosmic evil. Alignment does not force a player to act one way, but the GM can use it for story consequences.

4. Power System

Power Chance Wheel

Before getting powers, a player may spin the Power Chance Wheel. If powers are granted, the player may continue to the Power Count Wheel. If no powers are granted, the hero has no powers and must rely on stats, skill, equipment, and teamwork.

Power Count Wheel

The Power Count Wheel decides how many powers the hero gets. Current power count chances:

  • 1 Power: 37%
  • 2 Powers: 35%
  • 3 Powers: 10%
  • 4 Powers: 8%
  • 5 Powers: 5%
  • 6 Powers: 2%
  • 7 Powers: 3%

Power Wheel

After power count is decided, the player spins the Power Wheel once for each power. The same power cannot be gained twice on the same hero. If the player does not manually spin all powers, Generate Entire Hero fills in the missing powers.

Power Rarity Rule

Basic powers should be more common. Extremely powerful abilities should be rare. For future versions, powers can be divided into Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary, and Nexus-tier. The GM should be careful with reality warping, time travel, immortality, and universe-level powers.

5. Stat System

Every hero has seven main stats:

  • Strength: physical force and lifting power.
  • Speed: movement speed and reaction speed.
  • Durability: toughness and damage resistance.
  • Intelligence: knowledge, invention, and planning.
  • Combat: fighting skill and battle technique.
  • Stamina: endurance and how long the hero can keep fighting.
  • Power Control: how well the hero controls abilities.

Stat Levels

Stats range from 1 to 14. Level 1 is weak or uncontrolled. Level 14 is god-tier or absolute. The higher the total score, the higher the hero rank.

Power Score

Power Score is the total of all seven stat numbers. Minimum score is 7. Maximum score is 98. Power Score is used for ranking and quick battles.

6. Ranks

  • 7-20: Street Hero
  • 21-35: Enhanced Hero
  • 36-50: Super Hero
  • 51-65: Champion
  • 66-80: Omega Hero
  • 81-95: Cosmic Hero
  • 96+: Nexus Ascendant

7. Archetypes

The hero's highest stat decides their archetype.

  • Highest Speed: Speedster
  • Highest Strength: Powerhouse
  • Highest Durability: Tank
  • Highest Intelligence: Genius
  • Highest Combat: Martial Master
  • Highest Stamina: Endurance Hero
  • Highest Power Control: Energy Master

8. Hero Class

Hero Class is based on powers and archetype. For example, Telepathy may create a Telepath, Reality Warping may create a Reality Warper, and high Strength may create a Brute. Class helps the GM understand the hero's battlefield role.

9. Equipment System

Equipment Chance Wheel

The Equipment Chance Wheel decides if the hero starts with equipment. If equipment is not granted, the hero starts with none.

Equipment Count Wheel

If equipment is granted, spin Equipment Count to decide how many items the hero receives.

Equipment Wheel

Spin once for each item. Equipment can include weapons, armor, relics, shields, crystals, cores, and artifacts. Equipment may give roleplay advantages or battle bonuses if the GM allows it.

Suggested Equipment Bonuses

  • Minor weapon: +2 battle bonus
  • Strong weapon: +5 battle bonus
  • Armor: +3 defense bonus
  • Artifact or relic: +5 to +10 special bonus

10. Weakness System

Weakness Count Wheel

The Weakness Count Wheel decides how many weaknesses a hero may have. Current system gives 1, 2, or 3 weaknesses.

Weakness Wheel

Weaknesses are flaws, limits, or problems that make the hero more interesting. Examples include Energy Drain, Public Distrust, Fear, Overconfidence, and Power Instability.

Using Weaknesses

The GM should not use weaknesses to instantly defeat players. Weaknesses should create drama, tension, bad choices, and story consequences.

11. Team Rules

A team can currently hold up to 5 heroes. The Team Builder stores hero name, class, and rank. Teams can be used for team battles, campaigns, missions, and PvP.

Suggested Team Roles

  • Tank: protects the team.
  • Damage Dealer: deals heavy damage.
  • Speedster: acts first and rescues allies.
  • Support: heals, shields, or boosts.
  • Genius: solves problems and creates plans.
  • Leader: coordinates the team.

12. GM Portal

The GM Portal contains GM-only tools:

  • Villain Generator
  • Mission Generator
  • Battle Simulator
  • Rulebook

13. Villain Rules

Villains are generated with a name, species, origin, threat level, class, power score, and rank. The GM may use the generated villain as-is or edit their story manually.

Villain Rank

  • Street Villain
  • Enhanced Villain
  • Super Villain
  • Champion Villain
  • Omega Villain
  • Cosmic Villain
  • Nexus Villain

Threat Levels

  • Street Threat: local danger.
  • City Threat: threatens a city.
  • National Threat: threatens a country.
  • Planetary Threat: threatens the world.
  • Galactic Threat: threatens planets or star systems.
  • Universal Threat: threatens reality.
  • Nexus Threat: threatens the multiverse or the Nexus itself.

14. Mission Rules

The Mission Generator creates a mission and reward. The GM should connect the mission to the villain, team, and campaign.

Mission Examples

  • Bank Robbery
  • Hostage Rescue
  • Artifact Recovery
  • Alien Invasion
  • Dimensional Rift
  • Reality Breach
  • Nexus Energy Surge

Reward Examples

  • Credits
  • Rare Equipment
  • Legendary Equipment
  • Artifact
  • Power Upgrade
  • Bonus Stat Point
  • Team Resource

15. Battle System 1: Quick Battle

Quick Battle is the fastest way to resolve fights. This is what the current Battle Simulator uses.

Quick Battle Steps

  1. Generate a hero.
  2. Generate a villain.
  3. Click Battle Simulator.
  4. The system compares Hero Power Score + random bonus vs Villain Power Score + random bonus.
  5. Higher total wins.

Quick Battle Formula

Hero Total = Hero Power Score + random 0-19 bonus.
Villain Total = Villain Power Score + random 0-19 bonus.

Quick Battle Use

Use Quick Battle for fast fights, solo missions, testing heroes, or when you do not want a full turn-by-turn fight.

16. Battle System 2: Dice Battle

Dice Battle is for players who want more tabletop-style combat. Use a d20 or random number from 1 to 20.

Turn Order

Each character rolls:

Speed Stat + d20

Highest result acts first.

Attack Roll

Attacker rolls:

Combat Stat + d20

Defense Roll

Defender rolls:

Durability Stat + d20

Hit Rule

If Attack Roll is higher than Defense Roll, the attack hits. If Defense Roll is equal or higher, the attack misses or is blocked.

Damage Rule

Damage = Strength or Power Control + weapon bonus. The defender reduces damage using Durability.

Power Use

When using a power, roll:

Power Control + d20

The GM decides the difficulty:

  • Easy: 10
  • Medium: 15
  • Hard: 20
  • Extreme: 25
  • Nexus Level: 30+

Critical Success

Natural 20 means the action succeeds with a bonus effect.

Critical Failure

Natural 1 means the action fails badly or creates a complication.

17. Battle System 3: Full Campaign Battle

Full Campaign Battle is for long fights, team battles, boss fights, PvP, and major story missions.

Full Battle Setup

  1. Pick the hero team.
  2. Pick or generate the villain team.
  3. Choose mission objective.
  4. Choose battlefield.
  5. Roll turn order.
  6. Play round by round.

Round Structure

  1. Start of round effects happen.
  2. Fastest character acts first.
  3. Each character gets one main action.
  4. Each character may get one movement or minor action.
  5. End of round effects happen.

Main Actions

  • Attack
  • Use Power
  • Defend
  • Protect Ally
  • Use Equipment
  • Escape
  • Objective Action

Team Combo Rule

Two heroes may combine powers if the GM agrees. Both players roll Power Control. If both succeed, the combo gains a bonus.

Boss Fight Rule

Boss villains may act twice per round or gain a bonus reaction. Bosses should have higher Power Score, special resistances, or unique objectives.

Defeat Rule

A defeated hero is not always dead. They may be knocked out, captured, weakened, injured, or forced to retreat. Death should only happen if the group agrees.

18. Campaign Modes

Standard Campaign

Players form a team, fight villains, complete missions, gain rewards, and slowly rise in rank.

Endless Campaign

The campaign never truly ends. Threats keep getting stronger until the team reaches cosmic or Nexus-level enemies.

Power Campaign

The goal is to become the strongest hero, villain, or team in the universe. Players seek upgrades, relics, rare powers, and rank advancement.

Villain Campaign

Players may play anti-heroes or villains trying to conquer cities, planets, dimensions, or the Nexus itself.

PvP Campaign

Players may fight each other using Quick Battle, Dice Battle, or Full Campaign Battle. PvP should be agreed on before the campaign starts.

One-Shot Mode

Create heroes, generate one mission, fight one villain, and finish in one session.

Survival Mode

Heroes fight waves of enemies. Each wave gets harder. Rewards are earned after each wave.

19. Leveling And Progression

Suggested Level Rewards

  • Complete easy mission: +1 progress point
  • Complete hard mission: +2 progress points
  • Defeat major villain: +3 progress points
  • Complete campaign arc: +5 progress points

Spending Progress Points

  • 3 points: reroll one weakness
  • 5 points: gain +1 to one stat
  • 7 points: upgrade equipment
  • 10 points: gain a new power chance roll
  • 15 points: evolve a power

20. GM Advice

  • Do not punish players for bad rolls too harshly.
  • Use weaknesses to make stories better, not to ruin characters.
  • Let players describe how their powers look.
  • Make villains personal when possible.
  • Reward creative ideas.
  • Use high-tier powers carefully.
  • Let the team win sometimes and struggle sometimes.
